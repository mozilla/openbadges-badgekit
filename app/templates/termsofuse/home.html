Introduction

BadgeKit and the Mozilla Backpack are platforms for educators, nonprofits, and others that support Mozilla’s Open Badges Infrastructure for issuing, earning, and displaying “badges,” which are digital symbols of recognition.

The BadgeKit website and API are designed for badge “Issuers,” individuals or entities that create, publish, approve, and award badges. Issuers can create new badges from scratch or from existing templates. They can share draft badges with others for input and can publish the badges on third-party Issuer websites, where they become available to be earned. BadgeKit also provides an interface for Issuers to review badge earners’ badge applications and award badges. An “Earner” is anyone who earns a badge. Earners do not interface with the BadgeKit site; Earners apply for badges, submit “Evidence” content in support of their applications, and display their badges all on third-party Issuer sites. Earners may manage earned badges in “Badge Backpacks,” which are badge repositories and user management interfaces where badge Earners can collect, display, group, and delete badges from a variety of sources. The “Mozilla Backpack” is Mozilla’s own hosted instance of a Badge Backpack.
